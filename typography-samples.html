Hebrew Font Notes

  • HE-1 Frank Ruhl Libre + Heebo (Recommended) — Frank Ruhl is the closest Hebrew equivalent to Cormorant/Garamond: elegant, high-contrast serif. Heebo is Israel's most popular web sans-serif — excellent readability.
  • HE-2 Noto Serif Hebrew + Assistant — Noto is Google's universal serif. Assistant is very light and airy — good for minimalist luxury.
  • HE-3 Frank Ruhl Libre + Rubik — Same elegant serif, but Rubik is more geometric (like Inter) for a modern tech feel.
  • HE-4 Noto Serif Hebrew + Noto Sans Hebrew — Matched pair from Google. Most consistent, but less distinctive.

Arabic Font Notes

  • AR-1 Amiri + Cairo (Recommended) — Amiri is the gold standard Arabic serif — inspired by classic Naskh calligraphy. Cairo is Egypt's most popular modern sans-serif. Best luxury Arabic pairing.
  • AR-2 Noto Serif Arabic + Noto Sans Arabic — Google's universal pair. Very consistent but less character.
  • AR-3 El Messiri + Tajawal — El Messiri has a bold, distinctive display feel. Tajawal is light and clean. More modern/contemporary.
  • AR-4 Amiri + Tajawal — Classic serif + light modern. Hybrid of AR-1 warmth with AR-3 lightness.
